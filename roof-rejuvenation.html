How roof rejuvenation works

Asphalt shingles naturally dry out over time. As the oils leave the shingle, it can become more brittle, lose granules, and age faster in Michigan’s freeze/thaw swings. Roof rejuvenation is a spray-on treatment that helps replenish what aging shingles lose, helping them stay flexible and better protected.

  • Free roof evaluation + honest “qualify / not qualify” answer
  • Light prep and protection of landscaping as needed
  • Professional application for even coverage and clean results
  • Improves flexibility and helps reduce ongoing granule loss
  • Helps with algae/moss staining and slows regrowth
  • If it’s not a fit, we’ll quote repair or replacement options

Roof rejuvenation is best for aging asphalt shingles that are still laying flat and structurally sound. We’ll inspect your roof first and recommend the most cost-effective path.

Is your roof a good candidate?

Most rejuvenation projects are on roofs that show early signs of aging (granule loss, dry look, minor brittleness), but still have solid structure. If shingles are severely curled, missing, or the roof has major active leaks, you may need repair or replacement first. Either way, you’ll get a clear plan and pricing before any work begins.

Roof Rejuvenation FAQ

Quick answers for Lincoln Park homeowners comparing roof rejuvenation vs roof repair or roof replacement. If you want a second opinion, call (734) 224-5615.

What is roof rejuvenation?
Roof rejuvenation is a spray-on treatment for asphalt shingle roofs that helps restore flexibility and slow down the aging process. It’s a cost-effective option when your roof is worn but still structurally sound.
How long can roof rejuvenation extend my roof’s life?
Every roof is different, but many qualifying asphalt shingle roofs can gain additional years of service. Your results depend on roof age, ventilation, granule loss, and overall condition.
How do I know if my roof qualifies?
If shingles are laying mostly flat and the roof structure is sound, rejuvenation may be a fit. Severe curling, missing shingles, saturated decking, or major active leaks may require repair or replacement first.
Is roof rejuvenation cheaper than roof replacement?
Yes. Rejuvenation is typically a fraction of the cost of replacing a roof, which is why many homeowners use it to buy time and plan ahead instead of rushing into a full replacement.
Will roof rejuvenation stop leaks?
Rejuvenation is designed to help aging shingles perform better, but it’s not a substitute for proper roof repair. If you have an active leak, we’ll locate the cause and recommend the right repair plan.
Does roof rejuvenation help with algae or roof staining?
It can help reduce algae and organic growth and slow regrowth. We’ll tell you what’s realistic for your roof and whether a cleaning step is needed first.
Is roof rejuvenation safe for pets and landscaping?
We take precautions to protect your property during application. If you have concerns about pets, plants, or nearby gardens, tell us and we’ll plan accordingly.
What if my roof is too far gone?
If rejuvenation isn’t the right solution, we can quote roof repairs or a full roof replacement. You’ll get a clear recommendation and transparent pricing either way.
Can you help if insurance is involved?
Yes. If your roof damage is tied to wind or storm events, we can help document the condition and work with the insurance process so you understand your options.
Do you also do siding and gutters?
Yes. Lincoln Park Roofing also installs and repairs siding and gutters, so you can keep the entire exterior protected and looking sharp.

Still deciding?

If you’re seeing granules in your gutters, streaking, or a dry/brittle look, rejuvenation may be the affordable next step. If there’s storm damage, we can also help with insurance documentation and repair options.
