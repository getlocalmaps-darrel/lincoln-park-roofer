Roof Replacement in Lincoln Park, MI

March 03, 2026 · · By Scott
Lincoln Park Roofing completed a full roof replacement using GAF Timberline HDZ shingles after severe storm damage compromised this Lincoln Park home. Our crew finished the 1,800 square foot installation in 3 days, restoring complete protection against Michigan's harsh freeze-thaw cycles and wind-driven rain that frequently hits homes along the Ecorse Creek corridor.

Project Overview

The homeowner contacted us after a spring storm tore off 14 shingles and exposed the north-facing section of their roof to water infiltration. We discovered 3 areas of compromised decking near the chimney where water had penetrated for weeks before the homeowner noticed interior stains. Our crew replaced 32 square feet of damaged 5/8" OSB decking, installed GAF FeltBuster synthetic underlayment across the entire 18-square roof, and applied StormGuard ice and water barrier along all eaves and valleys—critical protection zones for Lincoln Park homes built in the 1950s and 1960s that line Fort Street. At Lincoln Park Roofing, we don't rush through decking inspections because solid substrate means your shingles will actually reach their 30-year lifespan. This roofer in Lincoln Park project increased the home's resale value by an estimated $12,000 according to local realtor comps and eliminated the moisture problems that threatened the attic insulation.

Challenges & Solutions

The existing decking showed significant depression in two sections where the original 1962 installation used undersized 1/2" plywood instead of the 5/8" thickness required by current Michigan building codes. We sistered additional plywood alongside the original rafters in these areas to create a level nailing surface—skipping this step would have created visible waves in the shingle pattern within 6 months. We also found that previous owners had installed bathroom vent ducts that terminated in the attic instead of extending through the roof, creating moisture accumulation that rotted the decking from below. Before we start any roof replacement in Lincoln Park, we video-document the entire driveway perimeter to capture all existing cracks and settlement patterns, protecting both our crew and the homeowner from disputes about vehicle access damage during material delivery.

Pro Tip from Lincoln Park Roofing: Install GAF StormGuard ice and water barrier at least 6 feet up from your eaves on all roof planes, not just the minimum 3 feet code requires. Lincoln Park's freeze-thaw cycles between December and March create ice dams that back water up 4 to 5 feet on ranch-style homes with shallow pitches—we've seen damage 54 inches above the gutter line on north-facing slopes near the Goddard Road area. Spending an extra $180 on extended coverage prevents $3,000 in ceiling repair bills.
