Emergency Roof Repair in Riverview Heights, Trenton, MI

March 01, 2026 · Riverview Heights, Trenton, MI · By Scott
We completed an emergency roof repair on a two-story colonial home in Riverview Heights, Trenton, MI after high winds tore off a 10x15 section of shingles. Our team tarped the exposed area within 4 hours of the call and completed full shingle replacement with CertainTeed Landmark the following morning, restoring weathertight protection before the next rainfall.

Project Overview

The homeowner called us at 2 PM on a Thursday after discovering a 10x15 section of shingles completely torn from the front slope of their colonial. Strong winds from a Lake Erie weather system had exposed the underlayment, creating immediate risk for water damage to the attic and upper floor. We arrived at 5:30 PM that same day, secured a heavy-duty tarp over the exposed section, and returned at 7 AM Friday to begin permanent repairs. We replaced all damaged shingles with CertainTeed Landmark shingles in Weathered Wood to match the existing roof, installed new galvanized step flashing where the roof meets the brick chimney, and hand-sealed every exposed nail head with roofing mastic. The entire repair took 6 hours from start to cleanup. At Lincoln Park Roofing, we know Trenton homeowners can't afford to wait days for emergency repairs—Michigan weather doesn't give you second chances.

Challenges & Solutions

The biggest challenge was preventing water intrusion before we could source matching shingles. We tarped the 10x15 exposed section with reinforced 6-mil poly secured with 2x4 battens—this kept the interior dry overnight while we confirmed the shingle color match. The second challenge involved the chimney flashing, which had deteriorated from years of freeze-thaw cycles common in Trenton's climate. We didn't just replace the damaged shingles—we upgraded the step flashing from aluminum to galvanized steel and added a continuous bead of through-the-tube sealant at every shingle overlap. Most homeowners assume emergency repairs mean quick patches, but we treat every project like a 20-year solution.

Pro Tip from Lincoln Park Roofing: After any wind event over 40 MPH, walk across the street and photograph your roof from ground level with your phone. Zoom in on valleys, chimneys, and ridge caps—these areas lose shingles first. If you spot any lifted edges or missing tabs, call us within 24 hours. A $650 repair today prevents a $4,500 interior damage claim next month. We keep CertainTeed Landmark and Owens Corning Duration in Weathered Wood, Driftwood, and Estate Gray in stock specifically for Riverview Heights emergencies—these three colors cover 80% of colonial roofs in Trenton.

About Riverview Heights

Riverview Heights sits in south Trenton between West Road and the Trenton Channel of the Detroit River, about 2 miles from Elizabeth Park. The neighborhood features 1950s-60s colonials and Cape Cods on tree-lined streets, most with 25-30 year old roofs approaching replacement age. Homes here face direct wind exposure from Lake Erie storm systems that funnel up the Detroit River, making shingle blow-offs more common than in sheltered Downriver neighborhoods. We've worked on 14 roofs in Riverview Heights over the past 3 years, and the combination of mature maples dropping branches and high-wind events means homeowners here need a roofer who responds the same day, not next week.
