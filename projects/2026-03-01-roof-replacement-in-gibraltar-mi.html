Lincoln Park Roofing completed a full roof redeck on a Gibraltar, MI home near the Lake Erie shoreline, installing Owens Corning Duration Flex shingles with continuous ridge venting and 12 baffles for proper attic airflow. Project Overview

We replaced a 22-year-old roof on a 1,800-square-foot colonial in Gibraltar's West Jefferson neighborhood after the homeowner noticed granule loss and three active leaks. The biggest challenge we faced was correcting inadequate ventilation—the original installation had zero soffit intake vents and only one gable vent, which caused premature shingle failure. We installed 18 SmartBaffles from Durovent to maintain proper airflow from soffit to ridge, then laid Owens Corning Duration Flex shingles rated for 130 MPH winds. Our crew added four Lomanco box vents for exhaust and retrofitted continuous soffit vents along the eaves. The project wrapped in 5 days despite a rain delay, and the homeowner immediately noticed lower attic temperatures. At Lincoln Park Roofing, we've completed over 200 roof replacements across Gibraltar and Downriver communities, and we know exactly how Lake Erie humidity impacts roof ventilation requirements.

Challenges & Solutions

The attic had zero insulation baffles and only 60 square feet of exhaust ventilation for a 1,200-square-foot attic space—half of what Michigan building code requires. We installed 18 Durovent SmartBaffles between each rafter bay to create clear air channels from soffit to ridge, preventing insulation from blocking intake airflow. Then we added four Lomanco box vents spaced evenly across the rear slope to hit the 1:150 ventilation ratio the code demands. Gibraltar sits less than a mile from Lake Erie, so moisture control isn't optional—it's the difference between a 20-year roof and a 12-year roof. We've seen dozens of Gibraltar homes with mold on roof decking because the original roofer skipped ventilation upgrades.

Pro Tip from Lincoln Park Roofing: Install at least 1 square foot of net free ventilation area for every 150 square feet of attic space, split 50/50 between intake (soffit) and exhaust (ridge or box vents). For a 1,200-square-foot attic, that means 8 square feet total—4 intake and 4 exhaust. Use Durovent SmartBaffles in every rafter bay to keep soffit vents clear, especially in homes with blown-in insulation. We measure this on every Gibraltar project because Lake Erie humidity will destroy an under-ventilated roof in 12-15 years instead of the 25-30 you should get from quality shingles.
