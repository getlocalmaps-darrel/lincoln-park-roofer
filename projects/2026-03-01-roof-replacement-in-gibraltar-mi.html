Roof Replacement in Gibraltar, MI

March 01, 2026 · pick a neighborhood, gibraltar, MI · By Scott
We completed a full roof replacement in Gibraltar's lakefront neighborhood, installing Owens Corning Duration shingles with a balanced ventilation system that includes intake baffles and ridge vents. The project addressed failing decking, outdated ventilation, and Michigan's freeze-thaw cycles that had compromised the original 20-year-old roof. This home is now protected for the next 30 years.

Project Overview

A Gibraltar homeowner near the Lake Erie shoreline contacted us after noticing interior water stains and ice dam damage from last winter. We stripped the old roof down to the decking and discovered 40% of the OSB sheathing needed replacement due to moisture infiltration. We installed 18 sheets of 7/16" OSB, then applied Owens Corning Duration shingles in Estate Gray to match the neighborhood's coastal aesthetic. The ventilation system was completely inadequate—just two small gable vents for a 2,400 square foot ranch. We installed 12 Owens Corning VentSure InFlow intake vents along the soffits and a continuous ridge vent to create proper airflow. At Lincoln Park Roofing, we run our own crews—no subcontractors—so the same team that started this job on Monday morning finished it Thursday afternoon. We've been the go-to roofer in Gibraltar for homeowners who want Michigan weather expertise, not a sales pitch.

Challenges & Solutions

The biggest challenge was the decking. We pulled off the old shingles and found soft spots across nearly half the roof where condensation had rotted the OSB from underneath. Gibraltar's location between Lake Erie and the Detroit River means higher humidity than communities 10 miles inland, and without proper ventilation, attic moisture has nowhere to go. We replaced 18 sheets of decking before laying down synthetic underlayment. The second issue was configuring intake ventilation on a home with narrow soffits—we used low-profile VentSure InFlow vents that fit the 2-inch soffit cavity while still delivering 9 square inches of net free area per vent. Most roofers skip this step and just slap shingles over bad wood. We don't work that way.

Pro Tip from Lincoln Park Roofing: In Gibraltar's humid lakefront climate, insist on intake vents every 18-24 inches along your soffits, not just a few scattered intakes. We use Owens Corning VentSure InFlow vents because each one delivers 9 square inches of net free area in a low-profile housing that fits tight soffits. Pair them with continuous ridge venting—not box vents—to create true convection airflow. This setup drops attic temperatures by 15-20 degrees in summer and eliminates the condensation that rots decking from below.

About pick a neighborhood

Gibraltar sits on a peninsula between the Detroit River and Lake Erie, with most homes built between 1950 and 1980 on streets like Lakeview Drive and Gibraltar Road. The lakefront location means homes face constant humidity, salt air corrosion on flashing, and punishing freeze-thaw cycles every winter. Many Gibraltar properties fall under the Gibraltar Shores Homeowners Association, which requires neutral shingle colors and prohibits architectural changes visible from the street without approval. We've worked with the HOA on dozens of roofs and know exactly what they'll approve. The soil here is clay-heavy with poor drainage, so ice dams are common—proper attic ventilation isn't optional, it's survival.
