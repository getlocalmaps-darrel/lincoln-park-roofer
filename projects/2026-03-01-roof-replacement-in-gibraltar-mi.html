Project Overview

A homeowner near Gibraltar Bay contacted us after discovering their 28-year-old roof had 3 layers of deteriorating shingles and zero ventilation baffles. We stripped all layers down to the original 5/8" plywood decking, replaced 14 sheets with CDX plywood where water damage had occurred, and installed 48 Durovent baffles to maintain airflow at the eaves. Our crew then applied Owens Corning Duration Flex shingles in Estate Gray, a color that complements Gibraltar's coastal cottage architecture. We added 16 roof-mounted intake vents and continuous ridge venting to meet Michigan's 1:150 ventilation code. The project took 4 days from tearoff to final cleanup. At Lincoln Park Roofing, we've handled over 200 waterfront roofs in Downriver communities, so we understand how Lake Erie's moisture patterns affect ventilation design.

Challenges & Solutions

The biggest issue we found was 3 layers of shingles installed over 28 years—each layer trapping more heat and moisture. We removed 42 squares of old material in one day, exposing rotted decking along the north-facing eave where ice dams had pushed water under the shingles for years. Too many homeowners believe ventilation is optional, but we see the proof every week: unventilated roofs in Gibraltar's humid climate fail 8 to 12 years early because trapped moisture rots the decking from below. That's why we installed Durovent baffles in every rafter bay and paired intake vents with ridge exhaust—Michigan building code requires it, and physics demands it.

Pro Tip from Lincoln Park Roofing: On Gibraltar's waterfront homes, we always install a double layer of ice and water shield—one course at the eave and a second course 3 feet up from the edge. Standard practice calls for one course, but Lake Erie ice dams can push water 18 to 24 inches higher than inland homes experience. We use GAF StormGuard or Owens Corning WeatherLock, both rated to seal around nail penetrations at temperatures down to -20°F. This upgrade costs $180 to $240 but prevents the $3,500 interior damage we see every winter when single-course installations fail during February thaws.

Residential Roofing Details

Roof replacement in Gibraltar starts with complete removal of existing shingles, inspection of roof decking for water damage, and installation of proper ventilation before any shingles go down. We install Owens Corning Duration or Duration Flex shingles, both rated for 130 mph winds and backed by 50-year limited warranties when paired with Owens Corning synthetic underlayment. Do I need a full roof replacement or just repair for damaged shingles in Gibraltar?
If your roof is over 18 years old or has multiple layers of shingles, we recommend full replacement rather than patching a few damaged areas. Gibraltar's humidity accelerates deterioration, so once you see curling or granule loss on 15% or more of the roof, the entire surface is near failure. Patching buys 1 to 2 years but costs $600 to $900—money better spent toward a new roof.
