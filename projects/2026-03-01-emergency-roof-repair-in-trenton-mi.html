Project Overview

A homeowner in Riverview Heights called us at 3 p.m. on a Tuesday after discovering their two-story colonial had lost a 10x15-foot section of shingles during an afternoon windstorm. We installed 47 CertainTeed Landmark shingles in Weathered Wood to match the existing roof, replaced 12 linear feet of aluminum step flashing around the chimney, and hand-sealed 183 nail heads with roof mastic. At Lincoln Park Roofing, we've handled 127 emergency repairs across Downriver Michigan since 2019. Challenges & Solutions

The biggest challenge was matching the existing Weathered Wood color on a roof installed in 2016. CertainTeed changed their dye lots slightly in 2018, so we pulled samples from three different supplier batches before finding shingles that blended seamlessly. We also discovered the original chimney flashing had been installed without a cricket, leaving standing water that accelerated rust. Instead of just patching what was there, we fabricated a 14-inch aluminum cricket to divert water around the chimney—a repair that'll add 8-12 years to the flashing's lifespan. Trenton's proximity to the Detroit River means we see 15% higher humidity than communities 5 miles inland, which speeds up metal corrosion. Every flashing job we do in Riverview Heights now includes corrosion-resistant coatings as standard.

Pro Tip from Lincoln Park Roofing: Keep 5-10 matching shingles in your garage after any roof installation or repair. We leave extras with every client, but store them inside—shingles left in direct sunlight for 2+ years become brittle and won't seal properly. If you're buying a home in Trenton with a roof older than 8 years, ask the seller for leftover shingles during negotiations. We've saved clients $200-$400 in color-matching fees when they have originals on hand.
