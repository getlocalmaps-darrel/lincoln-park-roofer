Our Full Tear-Off & Replacement Process

Every roof replacement we perform in Lincoln Park starts with a complete tear-off down to the decking. This allows us to inspect the plywood sheathing for rot, water damage, or structural issues that hide beneath old layers of shingles. Homes near Council Point Park and the Lincoln Park Historical Museum—many built in the 1940s through 1970s—often have original decking that needs targeted repairs before new materials go on. If you need certified roof repair instead of a full replacement, we can determine the right approach during our free inspection.

After decking verification, we install ice & water shield membrane along all eaves, valleys, and penetration points—critical protection in Michigan where ice dams are a recurring threat. A high-performance synthetic underlayment covers the remaining deck surface, creating a secondary water barrier beneath your shingles. We then install starter strip, GAF architectural shingles with proper offset patterns, and finish with ridge cap ventilation for balanced airflow. If recent storms caused the damage that led to your replacement, we also provide storm damage help and can guide you through the insurance claim process.

Shingle Comparison: 3-Tab vs. Architectural vs. Designer

Choosing the right shingle is one of the biggest decisions during a roof replacement. Here is how the three most common options compare for Lincoln Park homes.

Shingle Type Wind Rating Lifespan Best For
3-Tab 60–70 mph 15–20 years Budget-friendly projects, rental properties, and basic coverage needs.
Architectural (Dimensional) 110–130 mph 25–30 years Most Lincoln Park homes—excellent durability, clean appearance, strong warranty.
Designer (Premium) 130+ mph 30–50 years Maximum curb appeal and longevity—ideal for homeowners planning to stay long-term.

We recommend GAF Timberline HDZ architectural shingles for most Downriver homeowners. They deliver the best balance of cost, performance, and warranty protection for Michigan conditions.

Roof Replacement FAQs

Answers to the questions Lincoln Park homeowners ask most about roof replacement.

How much does a roof replacement cost in Lincoln Park?

Roof replacement costs vary based on roof size, pitch, shingle type, and whether structural repairs are needed. A typical Lincoln Park home ranges from $8,000 to $15,000 or more for premium materials. We provide free, detailed estimates with transparent pricing so you know exactly what to expect before work begins.

Do I need a full tear-off or can you install over existing shingles?

We recommend a full tear-off in most cases. Removing old shingles allows us to inspect the decking for hidden damage, install fresh underlayment and ice & water shield, and ensure proper ventilation. This approach meets Michigan building code requirements and delivers better long-term roof performance.

What shingles do you recommend for Michigan weather?

We typically recommend GAF Timberline HDZ architectural shingles for most Lincoln Park homes. They offer excellent wind resistance up to 130 mph, a dimensional appearance, and strong manufacturer warranties designed for Michigan's freeze-thaw cycles and severe weather patterns.

Does a new roof come with a warranty?

Yes. As GAF-certified installers, we offer manufacturer warranties on materials—including options for enhanced coverage through GAF's system warranty programs. We also provide a workmanship guarantee and walk you through every warranty detail before your project begins.

Can you help with insurance claims for roof replacement?

Yes. If storm damage triggered the need for replacement, we document the damage thoroughly, provide photos and scope details for your adjuster, and guide you through the claim process. Visit our insurance claim page for the full step-by-step guide.
