What Causes Roof Leaks in Michigan?

Ice dams form along Michigan eaves during freeze-thaw cycles, forcing meltwater beneath shingles and into the decking below. Wind-driven rain penetrates aging or improperly sealed shingle fields, especially after severe Downriver storms. Flashing failure around chimneys, vents, and roof valleys is the third leading cause, allowing steady water intrusion that damages insulation and interior ceilings over time.
