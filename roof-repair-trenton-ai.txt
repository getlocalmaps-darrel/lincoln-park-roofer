## Common Roof Repair Issues in Trenton Homes
- Active roof leaks: visible water stains on ceilings or attic decking
- Missing or curling shingles: often caused by wind gusts and aging 3-tab asphalt
- Damaged flashing: around chimneys, skylights, and roof-wall transitions where most leaks start
- Ice dam damage: shingle lifting and fascia rot from winter freeze-thaw cycles
- Storm-damaged valleys: torn underlayment and displaced ridge caps after high-wind events
- Failed pipe boots & vents: cracked rubber collars that drip directly into the attic

## Hail Damage Repair in Trenton
Trenton sits in the open Detroit River corridor with no elevation buffer to the west — when severe storms roll in from Lake Erie or down the I-75 / Telegraph corridor, Trenton catches some of the heaviest hail in Downriver. We see hail damage calls in Trenton at roughly 3x the rate of inland Wayne County cities. The damage often looks minor from the ground but is severe up close.

What hail damage actually does to a Trenton roof:
- **Bruised shingles** — granule loss exposes the asphalt mat; UV starts breaking it down within weeks
- **Cracked or fractured shingle backs** that won't show as leaks until the next 1–2 years of freeze-thaw
- **Dented metal flashing, vents, and gutters** — pinhole leaks at chimney, sidewall, and pipe penetrations
- **Compromised hidden water seals** even where the surface looks intact
- **Insurance-claim windows close fast** — most carriers require filing within 12 months of the storm
