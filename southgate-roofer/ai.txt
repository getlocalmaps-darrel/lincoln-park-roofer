These shortcuts cause problems 60-70 years later that a good roofer knows to check for.

2. **Ice dams are a recurring problem on Southgate roofs.** Low-slope ranch homes with wide eave overhangs collect ice that forces water under shingles. 3. **Original galvanized flashing is failing on 1960s-era homes.** Chimney flashing and plumbing vent boots from the original construction have corroded through after 60+ years. This is the most common hidden leak source on Southgate roofs. Lincoln Park Roofing replaces all flashing with modern aluminum on every replacement.

4. **Wind damage from open Downriver terrain.** Southgate sits on relatively flat, open land — wind loads during Michigan storms are higher than in tree-sheltered neighborhoods. Three-tab shingles from the 1990s and 2000s blow off in high winds. Lincoln Park Roofing installs architectural shingles rated for 130 mph winds.
